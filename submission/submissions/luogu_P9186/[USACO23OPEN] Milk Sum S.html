[USACO23OPEN] Milk Sum S

题目描述

Note: The time limit for this problem is 4s, 2x the default.

Farmer John’s NNN cows have integer milk production
values a1,…,aNa_1,\dots,a_Na1​,…,aN​. That is, the iii th cow produces aia_iai​ units of milk per
minute.

Each morning, Farmer John starts with all NNN cows hooked up to his milking
machine in the barn. He is required to unhook them one by one, sending them out
for their daily exercise routine. The first cow he sends out is unhooked after
just 1 minute of milking, the second cow he sends out is unhooked after another
minute of milking, and so on. Since the first cow (say, cow xxx) only spends
one minute on the milking machine, she contributes only axa_xax​ units of total
milk. The second cow (say, cow yyy) spends two total minutes on the milking
machine, and therefore contributes 2ay2a_y2ay​ units of total milk. The third cow
(say, cow zzz) contributes 3az3a_z3az​ total units, and so on. Let TTT represent the
maximum possible amount of milk, in total, that Farmer John can collect, if he
unhooks his cows in an optimal order.

Farmer John is curious how TTT would be affected if some of the milk production
values in his herd were different. For each of QQQ queries,
each specified by two integers iii and jjj, please calculate what would be the
new value of TTT if aia_iai​ were set to jjj. Note that
each query is considering a temporary potential change independent of all other
queries; that is, aia_iai​ reverts back to its original value before the next query
is considered.

输入格式

The first line contains NNN.

The second line contains a1…aNa_1\dots a_Na1​…aN​.

The third line contains QQQ.

The next QQQ lines each contain two space-separated integers iii and jjj.

输出格式

Please print the value of TTT for each of the QQQ queries on separate lines.

提示

For the first query, aaa would become [1,1,4,2,6][1,1,4,2,6][1,1,4,2,6], and
T=1⋅1+2⋅1+3⋅2+4⋅4+5⋅6=55T = 1 \cdot 1 + 2 \cdot 1 + 3 \cdot 2 + 4 \cdot 4 + 5 \cdot 6 = 55T=1⋅1+2⋅1+3⋅2+4⋅4+5⋅6=55.

For the second query, aaa would become [1,8,4,2,6][1,8,4,2,6][1,8,4,2,6], and
T=1⋅1+2⋅2+3⋅4+4⋅6+5⋅8=81T = 1 \cdot 1 + 2 \cdot 2 + 3 \cdot 4 + 4 \cdot 6 + 5 \cdot 8 = 81T=1⋅1+2⋅2+3⋅4+4⋅6+5⋅8=81.

For the third query, aaa would become [1,10,4,5,6][1,10,4,5,6][1,10,4,5,6], and
T=1⋅1+2⋅4+3⋅5+4⋅6+5⋅10=98T = 1 \cdot 1 + 2 \cdot 4 + 3 \cdot 5 + 4 \cdot 6 + 5 \cdot 10 = 98T=1⋅1+2⋅4+3⋅5+4⋅6+5⋅10=98.

1≤N≤1.5⋅1051\le N\le 1.5\cdot 10^51≤N≤1.5⋅105, 0≤ai≤1080 \leq a_i \leq 10^80≤ai​≤108,1≤Q≤1.5⋅1051\le Q\le 1.5\cdot 10^51≤Q≤1.5⋅105，0≤j≤1080 \leq j \leq 10^80≤j≤108.

  • Inputs 2-4: N,Q≤1000N,Q\le 1000N,Q≤1000.
  • Inputs 5-11: No additional constraints.
